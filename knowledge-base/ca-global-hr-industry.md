# CA Global HR — Industry Knowledge Base

## What the company does

CA Global HR provides HR outsourcing services to international companies that want to hire and manage employees in South Africa and across Africa without establishing a local legal entity. Their core offerings are:

- **Employer of Record (EOR):** CA Global HR becomes the legal employer of the client's South African staff. They handle employment contracts, payroll processing, tax and statutory deductions (PAYE, UIF, SDL), and compliance with South African labour law — while the client retains day-to-day management of the employees.
- **Professional Employer Organisation (PEO):** A co-employment model where the PEO shares employer responsibilities with the client company. The client has more direct involvement in HR management than in a pure EOR arrangement.
- **Contractor Payroll:** Payroll management, tax compliance, and payslip generation for contractors working in South Africa.

CA Global HR is a division of the broader CA Global group, which operates extensively across Africa. This gives them deep in-country expertise across multiple African jurisdictions, not just South Africa.

## The difference between EOR and PEO

This is the most common educational question in the space and a core content opportunity.

- **EOR:** The service provider is the sole legal employer. The client has no employment relationship with the worker under local law. Ideal for companies testing a new market, hiring one or two people, or needing to move fast without the cost of entity setup.
- **PEO:** A co-employment arrangement. The client and the PEO share employer responsibilities. Better suited to companies with established HR departments that want compliance support without fully outsourcing the employment relationship.

In South Africa, both models require deep knowledge of the BCEA, LRA, and associated statutory obligations.

## South African employment law context

Content about EOR and PEO in South Africa must be grounded in the actual legal landscape. Key legislation:

- **Basic Conditions of Employment Act (BCEA):** Sets minimum standards for working hours, leave, notice periods, and termination. Any employment contract must comply.
- **Labour Relations Act (LRA):** Governs collective bargaining, unfair dismissal, and dispute resolution via the CCMA (Commission for Conciliation, Mediation and Arbitration).
- **Employment Equity Act (EEA):** Requires designated employers to report on workforce demographics and implement affirmative action plans.
- **Skills Development Act & Skills Development Levies Act:** Companies with annual payroll above R500,000 must pay a 1% Skills Development Levy (SDL) to SETA (Sector Education and Training Authority).
- **UIF (Unemployment Insurance Fund):** Both employer and employee contribute 1% of remuneration (capped) to UIF monthly.
- **PAYE (Pay As You Earn):** Employers must withhold income tax from employee salaries and remit to SARS monthly.

Failure to comply with any of these obligations exposes companies to significant penalties and reputational risk in South Africa.

## Why international companies use EOR/PEO in South Africa

The most common use cases:
1. **Market entry without entity setup:** Registering a company in South Africa takes time and capital. EOR allows hiring to begin within days.
2. **Small headcount:** For 1-5 employees, the cost of setting up and maintaining a local entity rarely makes economic sense.
3. **Remote-first workforce:** Global companies hiring South African talent for remote roles who want full statutory compliance without local infrastructure.
4. **Project-based or fixed-term work:** Contractors or project teams where the employment relationship has a defined end.
5. **Compliance risk mitigation:** Companies that have been operating informally and want to regularise their position before a regulatory review.

- Authoritative and technically accurate — readers will fact-check against actual legislation
- Jargon-aware but not jargon-heavy — define acronyms on first use (BCEA, LRA, UIF)

- Africa-confident — lean into the regional expertise that global platforms cannot match

Avoid generic "HR solutions" language. Write as if the reader is a smart international HR director who has never hired in South Africa before and needs to get it right the first time.
