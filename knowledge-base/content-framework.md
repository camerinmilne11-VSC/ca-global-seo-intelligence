# Content Framework — CA Global Brands

## Pillar Page Structure
Each brand maintains 5–7 pillar pages covering major service/topic areas.
Pillar pages: 2,500–4,000 words, broad keyword focus, link to all cluster articles.
Cluster articles: 1,200–1,800 words, specific long-tail keyword, link back to pillar.

## Topic Cluster Strategy
Each cluster = one pillar page + 8–15 supporting articles.

Example clusters per brand:
- CA Global: Executive Search in Africa | Talent Acquisition Strategy | African Business Markets
- CA Mining: Mining Recruitment Africa | Technical Mining Roles | African Mining Markets
- CA Finance: Finance Recruitment Africa | CFO Hiring | African Finance Markets

## Candidate-Focused Content Framework
Objective: attract professionals considering a career move.
Structure: problem/situation → insight/data → actionable advice → CTA to register/connect
Examples: "How to Find Mining Jobs in South Africa", "What CFOs Earn in Sub-Saharan Africa"

## Client-Focused Content Framework
Objective: attract companies seeking recruitment support.
Structure: challenge/market context → our approach/expertise → proof/results → CTA to brief us
Examples: "How to Hire a Finance Director in Africa", "Building a Mining Leadership Team"

## Thought Leadership Framework
Objective: build authority and SEO visibility for high-value terms.
Structure: insight/observation → evidence/data → implications → our perspective → CTA
Examples: "The Rise of Battery Metals and African Mining Talent", "CFO Talent in Kenya: 2025 Outlook"

## Evergreen Content Framework
Objective: rank long-term, attract consistent organic traffic.
Structure: comprehensive answer → supporting detail → FAQ section → internal links
Examples: "Complete Guide to Executive Recruitment in Africa", salary guides, glossaries

## Location-Based Content Framework
Objective: rank for "[service] in [country/city]" searches.
Structure: market overview → our presence/network → common roles → client/candidate CTA
Examples: "Executive Recruitment in South Africa", "Mining Recruitment in Zambia"

## News/Commentary Framework
Objective: timeliness signals, topical authority.
Structure: news hook → context/analysis → implications for recruitment → CA perspective
Length: 600–900 words. Always include 2 internal links.
